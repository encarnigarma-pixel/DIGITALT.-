1. Welcome to our Travel Agency!

Hello, Digital Travelers! Welcome to your official training ground as international travel agents. In this project, you are going to work in teams to plan and promote an incredible trip to an English-speaking country.

As expert travel agents, you will need to research your destination's culture, famous landmarks, traditional food, and currency. To convince your clients to buy your travel package, you will create two amazing products:

-An Interactive Poster using Canva for Education.
-A Video-Vlog Campaign using Flip.


But remember! A great travel agent is also a safe traveler. Before we start surfing the web for information, we must secure our digital equipment and prepare our digital passport.

Mission 1: Preparing our Digital Passport

1.- Mission 1: Preparing our Digital Passport


Before we start searching for information about our English-speaking country on the internet, we need to make sure we are safe digital citizens. Follow these essential guidelines to protect your identity and respect others while working on your travel campaign:

  1. Safe Registration
Use a Nickname: When browsing travel forums, booking simulators, or public websites, never use your real name or surname. Create a cool traveler alias!

 
 Password Pro: Create strong passwords using secret phrases and symbols. Never share your passwords with your classmates, only with your parents.

 
 
 Privacy First: If a travel app or website asks for your live location or contacts, say NO! Only provide the strictly necessary data.

 2. Your Digital Footprint
 

Respect your Team: Before recording a teammate for your Flip video-vlog, always ask for permission. Their image belongs to them!

 
 
 

Think Before You Post: Everything you upload to the internet stays there. Be polite, practice good Netiquette (digital manners), and look after your digital reputation.

 
 
 3. Respect the Author
 

Google is not Free: The beautiful pictures of landscapes and the background music you find online have owners.

 
 
 

Use Free Databases: Look for images and audio in copyright-free websites like Pixabay or Pexels. Always check for Creative Commons licenses.

 
 
 

Give Credit: Remember to cite the author or website in your project’s final credits.

Mission 2: Creating the Travel Campaign

Introducción




Now that our Digital Passport is ready, it is time to work as real travel agents! Your team needs to create a fantastic campaign to promote your chosen English-speaking country. To do this, we will complete two main tasks.

Choose your role wisely and use your best strengths to help your team!

Keep it dynamic: Use clear pronunciation, expressive body language, and high energy!

Duración:

Be creative: Use professional templates, match nice colors, and organize the text so it is easy and fun to read for your clients.

Duración:
01:00

Record short, engaging video-messages using Flip to explain your travel recommendations orally.

Mission 3: In Case of Emergency (Plan B)

1. Technical Troubleshooting

Stay Calm: If a website or app freezes, try refreshing the page ($F5$) or checking your device's internet connection.
Safety Stop: If a pop-up window asks for strange digital permissions, suspicious data, or payment information, STOP immediately and report it to your teacher.
Ask a Peer: Before calling the teacher, ask your team mates. Sometimes they have already solved the exact same issue!

2. Our Paper Plan B

If the technology completely fails in our classroom, the digital travel agency does not stop! Your hard work is always safe.

The Paper Logbook: We will immediately switch to our physical notebooks or paper logbooks.
Keep Moving Forward: Use this time to write down your texts in English, design hand-drawn sketches of your Canva poster, or practice your Flip script with your partner face-to-face.

2. Inclusive Education (DUA Approach)

Universal Design for Learning (UDL / DUA)

This project is fully adapted to the Universal Design for Learning (UDL / DUA) guidelines by providing Multiple Means of Expression:

Students with difficulties in written expression or graphic design can lead the oral communication in Flip.
Students who face barriers in oral interaction or public speaking can focus on the research and visual design in Canva.
This balance ensures that every "Digital Traveler" can contribute to the team's final product using their personal strengths.

3. Classroom Recommendations & Technical Solutions

To achieve digital resilience in the classroom (Indicator 3.1.B2.3), teachers should consider the following solutions to common technical problems:

Wi-Fi or Device Failure: If connection fails, do not stop the class. Students must use their physical Paper Logbooks to sketch the poster designs, draft their written descriptions, or practice their speaking scripts face-to-face.
Privacy & Security Pop-ups: If a platform prompts an unexpected data request, instruct students to activate the Emergency Protocol: stop browsing immediately and notify the teacher.
Low Tech Barrier: Both Canva for Education and Flip are web-based, cloud-saving, and completely free. This eliminates software installation barriers and ensures students can resume their work from any device.
